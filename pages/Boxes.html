Box World

Dragons are naturally dangerous and luckily unecessary to continue to do maths.
When Gnurter first developed her Dragon Maths her hands acted as the dragons, adding, taking away and swapping the ruby and lapus lazuli stones as needed.
To help in counting she made up boxes containing ten, hundred and a thousand red or blue stones.
To show two hundred and forty three she would put into a bigger box two Hundred boxes, four Ten boxes and three One boxes.
From this idea she developed a new world of maths in which she imagined a vast store in which there where boxes containing each and every number there is.
The boxes she thought would keep the number stones apart until the time came to combine them into their result.
This world was populated by beings who would fetch and parcel numbers as required. For consistency she called these beings Dragons, though they appeared very human.
When a mathematician came to the counter and plasced an order for numbers these Dragons would go and fetch them from the store and add them to the counter.
These Dragons never took away any numbers for they always used the swap rules to work out which colour of number to add.
If the order was for –3 then they would fetch and add blue Three.

Here an order for 6–3x5 is brought to the counter

Here is how the Dragons would work out what to bring.
Every add + or take away – starts a new instruction so split orders into separate intructions.
By using swap for each negative sign work out the colour for each instruction.
Using the numbers in each instruction work out the result without colour.
Combine the colour and result to fetch the correct number box to the counter.
