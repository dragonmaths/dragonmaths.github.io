It all depends on what you are counting.
The first rule of counting is that counting only makes sense if you are counting the same thing.
As adding is just a change in the count the first rule of adding is you can only add counts of the same thing.

There are two hens in the picture.
There is one cockerel in the picture.
What do you get if you add two hens and one cockerel? Two hens and one cockerel.

When counting different objects if you can find something they have in common and rename them then you can count again and add them.
In this case they are chickens.
There are two chickens in the picture and one more chicken.
Two chickens add one chicken gives three chickens.

There are four cows in the picture.
There are two sheep in the picture.
What do you get if you add the four cows and the two sheep? You get four cows and two sheep.
What is the common denominator for cows and sheep? They are animals and four animals add two animals give six animals. However lots of others in the picture are also animals so there are more than six animals in the picture.

Numbers are the same. You need to be counting the same numbers to be able to add them.
You can add 3 Twos and 5 Twos to give 8 Twos
However
You cannot add 3 Fives and 2 Threes directly but need to find what is the common denominator of Fives and Threes.
The common denominator is Ones.
