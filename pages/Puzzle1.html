The mathematical way of stating the 12 is in the 4 times table is to say that 12 is a multiple of 4
So 12 is a multiple of 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 and 12

Any number in a multiplication table is a multiple of the number whose times table it is.
So in the 3 times table 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18 are all multiples of 3

Where is 12 in the 4 times table?
As three Fours are 12 then 12 is the third multiple.

Since 3 x 4 = 4 x 3 then 12 must be a multiple of 3
3 and 4 are called factors of 12

In a times table of a number that number is a factor of all the numbers in the table.
In the 3 times table 3 is a factor of 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18

Factors come in pairs.
For example 6 is a factor of 42 as 42 is in the 6 times table.
So 42 must be a multiple of 6
42 is seven Sixes.
42 = 7x6 = 6x7 so 42 is six Sevens.
42 is also a multiple of 7 and 7 is a factor 42
6,7 are a factor pair of 42
