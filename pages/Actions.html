Actions and results

Usually actions and the results of actions are seen as completely different. This is also true in the Domain of Numbers. For example the actions of adding and taking away are separate and different from the results of these actions.

The action adding One

is different from the result

The action taking away One

is different from the result

The actions of adding Two then adding Three

is different from the action adding Five

yet both have the same result

However when writing Mathematics actions and their results are often treated as the same thing and actions and their results are put to be equal.
In Mathematics
+2+3
2+3
+5
are all different ways in which 5 can be written as they all have the same result 5
Where actions have the same result they are equal and are equal to the result.
+2+3 = 2+3 = +5 = 5

In the above example it is well known that the actions +2+3 result in 5.
